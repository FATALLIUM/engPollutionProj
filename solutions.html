Good ol’ PM2.5, Diseases, and Disproportionate Exposure– All in New York



Possible solutions

Reducing PM2.5 emissions requires dedication and proper enforcement. Ways to do this are shown below:

  • Local Law 97 (Climate Mobilization Act):

    • Passed in April 2019 as part of the NYC Green Deal.

    Aims for netzero emissions in the city's largest buildings by 2050, with stricter limits by 2030.

    Buildings are penalized $268 per metric tonne of CO2 over the emission limit.

    As buildings reduce fossil fuel use, PM2.5 levels also decrease (Urban Green Council).


  • NYS Clean Heat Program:

    • Focuses on transitioning to cleaner energy, such as heat pumps.

    Greater impact observed in lower-income areas (Zhang et al.).

  • Zero Emission Vehicles (ZEVs):

    • Federal Clean Air Act allows NY to adopt California’s ZEV standards, including battery-electric and hydrogen fuel-cell vehicles.




    ZEVs

    • Produce no engine exhaust.
    • Emit no nitrogen oxides or hydrocarbons.
    • Costs around $24,000-$40,000
    • Have lower maintenance costs, saving up to $6,400 over time
    • ("Zero Emission Vehicles: Driving towards the Future").
